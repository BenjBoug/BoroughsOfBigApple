Queens

Presentation

Queens coincides with the Queens County. In 2010, its population was 2,230,722 inhabitants. Created on 1st November 1683 he was named in honor ofQueen Catherine of Braganza, wife of King Charles II of England. The total area of the county is 461 km2, of which 282.9 km2 of land area and 178.8 km2 of water. It is an area of thelargest boroughs of New York. Queens is connected to Manhattan by the Queensboro Bridge. The only road access to Roosevelt Island is from Queens by a bridge to the smaller end of the 36th Ave.

The borough of Queens occupies part of Long Island and the borough of Brooklyn. It also includes other small islands, mostly located in Jamaica Bay.

Originally, Queens County included an adjacent area now covers Nassau County. This is one of 12 counties created at the same time as the State of New York in 1683. Before1870, the Queens County had six towns: Newtown, Flushing, Jamaica, NorthHempstead, Hempstead and Oyster Bay. In 1870 the City of Long Island (Long IslandCity) was incorporated there. Long Island City, Newtown, Flushing, Jamaica, and Rockaway Peninsula portion of Hempstead became the borough of Queens in New-York on 1 January 1898. Part of Queens County that was not annexed to the City of New-York formed the Nassau County in 1899.
