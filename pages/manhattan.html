Manhattan is the most dense and richest of New York, making it the borough's most touristy Big Apple. In 2010, its population was 1,585,873 inhabitants and a density much higher than any other county in the United States. Manhattan is worldfamous for its skyscrapers and bustling activity.

Manhattan and the economic and financial heart of the city, built around Wall Street that houses the New York Stock Exchange, and has several Midtown headquarters of companies like Time Warner, Bloomberg or MetLife. It is also the cultural heart of New-York, with museums like the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the American Museum of Natural History, and MoMA (The Museum of Modern Art). Most of the skyscrapers of the most famous are also located in this district, for example with the Empire State Building, Chrysler Building, or the GE Building and the World Trade Center, symbol of economic power, which was destroyed in attacks of September 11, 2001.
